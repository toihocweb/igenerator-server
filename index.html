30 Days Without Social Media

Social media can be a timesuck and ruin productivity. It can even have a negative effect on mental health.

It’s all Tik Tok’s fault. Okay, okay, it’s mine. I downloaded Tik Tok to see what my kids were raving about. That was years ago. It’s now March 2022, and I have been spending three to four hours a night on Tik Tok alone. It’s embarrassing for me to admit that, but it’s true. That time doesn’t include the time during the day when I sporadically check social media. That’s a lot of time every single day wasted scrolling.

I’ve deleted Twitter, Facebook, and Tik Tok from my phone. I’m keeping Discord so I can chat with my daughter. Read on to see how my social media fast goes!

Day 1: I keep picking up my phone and looking at it out of habit. Habits are so powerful. I’m imagining what I could do with good habits. I feel a bit freer not spending all that time on my social media apps. I’ve noticed my mind feels quieter and calmer. It’s nice.

Day 2: I’m a bit bored when I would usually be scrolling through social media after dinner and before grocery shopping. I printed some more crossword puzzles to have on hand at night time. I also started listening to an audiobook last night (Billy Summers by Stephen King). I prepped the week’s smoothies and cold brew and made peanut butter bars. That kept me busy for about an hour. I still feel that serenity without social media. It reminds me of when I stopped reading the news for a few years because it was stressing me out. It seems like social media may be worse for my mental health than the news. I am looking forward to spending more time on things I enjoy, like spending time with my husband, cleaning, reading, exercising, writing, and watching horror movies.
